libya

The State of Libya is an Arab country located in North Africa, bordered by the Mediterranean Sea to the north, Egypt to the east, Sudan to the southeast, Chad and Niger to the south, and Algeria and Tunisia to the west. With an area of nearly 1.8 million square kilometers (700,000 square miles), Libya is the fourth largest country in Africa, the 17th largest country in the world, and ninth among the ten countries with the largest proven oil reserves for a country in the world.

Libya spans an area of 1,759,540 square kilometers (679,362 square miles), making it the 17th largest country in the world in terms of area. It is somewhat smaller than Indonesia in land area, and roughly the size of the US state of Alaska. It is bordered to the north by the Mediterranean Sea, to the west by Tunisia and Algeria, to the southwest by Niger, to the south by Chad and Sudan, and to the east by Egypt. Libya lies between latitudes 19° and 34°N, and longitudes 9° and 26°E. The Libyan coast is the longest of any African country on the Mediterranean Sea. So it is 1,770 kilometers (1,100 miles) long. Where there is a part of the Mediterranean Sea north of Libya called the Libyan Sea. The prevailing climate in most areas of the country is dry and between desert and semi-desert. An exception to this is the northern regions, which enjoy a temperate climate, which is the Mediterranean climate. Natural hazards come in the form of hot, dry seasonal currents, and sandstorms known in Libya as (Qibli). It lasts from one to four days during spring and fall. Oases are also spread throughout Libya, the most important of which are in Ghadames and Kufra.

The capital of Libya is Tripoli, which is now the largest city in the country. It is located in western Libya and is home to more than one million people out of the country's total population of more than six million. The second largest city is Benghazi in eastern Libya with a population of 700,000.
